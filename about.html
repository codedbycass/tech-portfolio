Learn more about me

As the child of Lao refugees, I wasn’t given a blueprint on how to navigate success in the US. Although the ambiguity scared me, I took it as an invitation to nurture my curiosities and freedoms.

In my previous role, I developed a successful digital equity program for immigrants and refugees. I quickly learned that elders want to learn technology, but the existing programs weren’t built with them in mind. I was enamored by the idea that I can leave a bigger impact by learning how to make effective and inclusive websites.

My background in social work and liberal arts informs my software engineering approach: thinking carefully, writing clean syntax, communicating effectively, and putting people first. My goal is to create inclusive technologies that empower socially and economically marginalized individuals to meaningfully participate in the digital and material world.
